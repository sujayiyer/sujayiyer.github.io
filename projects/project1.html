EV Thermal Management

Date: April 2025

ANSYS Simulation EV Thermal Systems

This project focused on optimizing the thermal performance of electric vehicle battery packs using CFD simulations. I created a parametric model in ANSYS Fluent to evaluate various cooling designs and airflow strategies under dynamic load conditions.

🔧 Objectives

  • Reduce hotspot temperature above 45°C
  • Evaluate air- vs liquid-cooled architectures
  • Simulate thermal performance under WLTP driving cycle

📊 Approach

The CAD model was imported into ANSYS Workbench and meshed using a hybrid tetrahedral method. Heat generation was simulated using battery discharge profiles, and boundary conditions were based on ambient operating scenarios from -10°C to +40°C.

✅ Results

  • Achieved 12% improvement in cooling uniformity
  • Reduced thermal gradient across cells by 7°C
  • Liquid-cooled loop met target thresholds under all cycles

🧠 Tools Used

ANSYS Fluent, Workbench, Python for pre-processing, Excel for result aggregation.
